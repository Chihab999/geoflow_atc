
ATC-20 Postearthquake Safety Evaluation of Buildings - Core Criteria Summary

1. INSPECTED (Green Placard)
Description: No apparent hazard found, although repairs may be required. Original seismic-resisting capacity is not significantly decreased. No restriction on use or occupancy.
Structural Criteria: 
- Minor or no cracking in structural concrete/masonry.
- No significant leaning or out-of-plumb walls.
- Minor architectural damage (e.g., plaster flaking, minor glass breakage).
- No damage to primary gravity or lateral force-resisting systems.

2. RESTRICTED USE (Yellow Placard)
Description: Hazardous condition exists or is believed to exist that requires a restricted use. Entering is permitted only by authorized personnel or under specific constraints.
Structural Criteria:
- Moderate structural damage to lateral load-resisting system.
- Noticeable wall cracking or spalling of concrete.
- Damage to non-structural elements that pose falling hazards (e.g., parapets, heavy facades).
- Main structural frame remains basically intact but exhibits signs of yielding.
- Building still supports gravity loads but lateral capacity is degraded.

3. UNSAFE (Red Placard)
Description: Extreme hazard, may collapse. Imminent danger of collapse from an aftershock. Do not enter.
Structural Criteria:
- Severe structural damage.
- Obvious leaning / out-of-plumb exceeding tolerances (e.g., > 1-2 degrees).
- Failure of connections between structural elements.
- Collapsed stories or partial collapse (pancaking).
- Severe shear cracking in vertical elements (X-cracks in columns/walls).
- Unsafe foundation displacement or severe liquefaction effects.
